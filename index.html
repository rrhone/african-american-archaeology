Centering Black Histories Through Archaeology

This site introduces African American archaeology, a field that uses material remains, documents, and community knowledge to tell stories that written records often silence or distort.

Archaeologists working in this tradition study homes, plantations, cemeteries, military posts, and urban neighborhoods connected to Black communities. They focus on everyday objects, architecture, and landscapes to understand how people created family, culture, and resistance under slavery and beyond.
